Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about PCs and laptops.

General Questions

Do I need a dedicated GPU?

It depends on your use case. For basic office work, web browsing, and streaming, integrated graphics are sufficient. However, if you plan to play modern games, edit videos, work with 3D modeling, or use graphics-intensive software, a dedicated GPU is essential. Gaming and professional creative work require at least a mid-range dedicated GPU like GTX 1650 or better.

How much RAM is enough?

8GB RAM is the minimum for basic use like web browsing and office work. 16GB is recommended for most users as it provides smooth multitasking and handles modern applications well. 32GB or more is needed for professional work like video editing, 3D rendering, running virtual machines, or heavy multitasking. For gaming, 16GB is the sweet spot for most modern games.

Is SSD worth it?

Absolutely yes! An SSD is one of the most impactful upgrades you can make. It makes your computer boot in seconds instead of minutes, programs open instantly, and overall system responsiveness improves dramatically. Even a budget laptop with an SSD will feel faster than a more powerful laptop with an HDD. Always choose SSD over HDD for your primary storage.

What is the difference between laptop and desktop?

Laptops are portable, have built-in batteries, and are all-in-one devices perfect for mobility. However, they are harder to upgrade and may have thermal limitations. Desktops are stationary, more powerful for the same price, easier to upgrade, have better cooling, and offer more customization options. Choose laptops for portability and desktops for maximum performance and value.

Buying Decisions

Should I buy a new or refurbished PC?

New PCs come with full warranty, latest technology, and peace of mind. Refurbished PCs can offer great value if bought from reputable sellers, but check warranty terms carefully. For critical work, buy new. For budget-conscious buyers or learning purposes, certified refurbished from trusted brands can be a good option. Always test thoroughly before purchase.

Which brand is best for laptops in Nepal?

Lenovo, HP, and Dell are the most popular and reliable brands in Nepal with good after-sales support. Lenovo offers the best value for money. HP is popular for office use. Dell is known for reliability. Asus is great for gaming. Choose based on your budget, requirements, and availability of service centers in your area.

What should I check before buying a laptop?

Check: 1) CPU generation (latest is better), 2) RAM amount and upgradeability, 3) Storage type (must be SSD), 4) Display quality (Full HD minimum, IPS panel preferred), 5) Battery capacity and real-world reviews, 6) Build quality and keyboard comfort, 7) Port selection (USB-C, HDMI), 8) Warranty terms and service center availability, 9) Weight if portability matters.

Is it better to buy online or from a shop?

Both have advantages. Online offers better prices and more options, but you cannot test before buying. Physical shops let you see and test the product, get immediate possession, and easier warranty claims. For expensive purchases, visiting a shop is recommended. For budget items with good reviews, online can save money. Always buy from authorized dealers for warranty validity.

Technical Questions

What is the difference between SSD and HDD?

SSD (Solid State Drive) uses flash memory with no moving parts, making it much faster, silent, more durable, and energy-efficient. HDD (Hard Disk Drive) uses spinning magnetic platters, making it slower, noisier, more fragile, but cheaper per GB. SSDs are 5-10x faster for boot times and file access. Always choose SSD for your operating system and frequently used programs.

What is the difference between Intel and AMD processors?

Both are excellent. Intel traditionally had better single-core performance, ideal for gaming. AMD Ryzen offers better multi-core performance and value for money, great for productivity and multitasking. Current generation: Intel 12th/13th gen and AMD Ryzen 5000/7000 series are very competitive. For most users, equivalent tiers (i5 vs Ryzen 5, i7 vs Ryzen 7) perform similarly. Choose based on price and availability.

Can I upgrade my laptop RAM and storage?

It depends on the laptop model. Many laptops allow RAM and storage upgrades through accessible panels. However, some modern thin laptops have soldered RAM that cannot be upgraded. Before buying, check if RAM slots are accessible and if there are extra M.2 slots for storage expansion. Always verify upgrade options in the laptop specifications or ask the seller before purchase.

What is integrated vs dedicated graphics?

Integrated graphics are built into the CPU, share system RAM, and are sufficient for basic tasks, video playback, and light gaming. Dedicated graphics (GPU) have their own memory (VRAM), much more powerful, and essential for gaming, video editing, 3D work, and graphics-intensive tasks. Integrated graphics save power and cost, while dedicated GPUs provide significantly better performance.

Performance & Usage

How can I make my old PC faster?

Top upgrades: 1) Replace HDD with SSD (biggest impact), 2) Add more RAM if you have less than 8GB, 3) Clean dust from fans and vents, 4) Reinstall Windows for a fresh start, 5) Disable startup programs, 6) Update drivers, 7) Use lightweight antivirus, 8) Consider upgrading to an SSD if you have not already - this single upgrade can make an old PC feel new.

What specs do I need for gaming?

Minimum for 1080p gaming: Intel i5/Ryzen 5, 16GB RAM, 512GB SSD, GTX 1650 or better. Recommended for smooth gaming: Intel i7/Ryzen 7, 16GB RAM, 1TB SSD, RTX 3060 or better. For competitive gaming, also consider 144Hz+ display. Always check specific game requirements. Modern AAA games need more powerful GPUs like RTX 4060 or higher for high settings.

What specs do I need for video editing?

Minimum: Intel i5/Ryzen 5, 16GB RAM, 512GB SSD, dedicated GPU with 4GB VRAM. Recommended: Intel i7/Ryzen 7, 32GB RAM, 1TB+ SSD, RTX 3060 or better. For 4K editing: i9/Ryzen 9, 64GB RAM, 2TB SSD, RTX 4070+. Fast storage is crucial - use NVMe SSD. More RAM allows working with larger projects. GPU accelerates rendering and effects.

How long should a laptop battery last?

New laptops typically last 4-8 hours for basic use, 2-4 hours for heavy use like gaming. Battery life depends on: screen brightness, running applications, battery capacity (Wh), and power settings. Over time, batteries degrade - expect 80% capacity after 2-3 years. To extend battery life: reduce brightness, close unused apps, use power-saving mode, avoid extreme temperatures, and do not keep it plugged in 24/7.

Technical Glossary

Quick reference guide to common tech terms.

CPU

Central Processing Unit

The "brain" of the computer that handles instructions.

GPU

Graphics Processing Unit

Specialized circuit for rendering images and video.

RAM

Random Access Memory

Short-term memory for active tasks.

SSD

Solid State Drive

Fast storage device with no moving parts.

HDD

Hard Disk Drive

Traditional storage with spinning magnetic platters.

BIOS

Basic Input/Output System

Firmware used to perform hardware initialization.

Overclocking

Overclocking

Running a component at a higher speed than factory settings.

Hz

Hertz (Refresh Rate)

Number of times per second a display updates its image.

Resolution

Resolution

The number of pixels contained on a display monitor.

Thermal Throttling

Thermal Throttling

Slowing down performance to reduce heat.

Benchmark

Benchmark

Standardized test to measure computer performance.

OS

Operating System

Software that manages computer hardware and software resources.
