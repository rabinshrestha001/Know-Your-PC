Ultimate PC Buying Guide

Follow this simple 4-step guide to choose the right computer for your needs. We break down the specs so you don't have to guess.

Step 1

Choose Your Purpose

Understanding how you will use the computer is the first step.

Student

Browsing, docs, online classes.

  • Portable
  • Good Battery
  • Budget Friendly

Office/Work

Excel, Zoom calls, multitasking.

  • Reliable
  • Fast SSD
  • Clear Webcam

Gaming

Playing high-end video games.

  • Powerful GPU
  • High Refresh Rate
  • Good Cooling

Editing/Creative

Video editing, 3D design, coding.

  • High RAM (16GB+)
  • Color Accurate Screen
  • Powerful CPU
Step 2

Minimum Specs Table

Minimum recommended specs for 2024-2025.

User Case CPU (Intel/AMD) RAM Storage GPU Needed?
Student i3 / Ryzen 3 8GB 256GB SSD No
Office i5 / Ryzen 5 16GB 512GB SSD No
Gaming i5 / Ryzen 5 (H-Series) 16GB 1TB SSD Yes (RTX 3050+)
Editing i7 / Ryzen 7 32GB 1TB NVMe Yes (RTX 4060+)
Step 3

Laptop vs Desktop

Choose the form factor based on your lifestyle.

Laptop

For those on the move

  • Portable: Take it anywhere.
  • All-in-One: Screen included.
  • Battery: Work unplugged.
  • Hard to upgrade.
  • Can get hot/loud.
VS

Desktop PC

Maximum power & value

  • Powerful: Higher performance.
  • Upgradable: Swap parts easily.
  • Cooling: Runs quieter.
  • Not portable.
  • Needs monitor/keyboard.
Step 4

Final Checklist

Before you pay, double-check these important factors.

Performance

Is the CPU generation current? (e.g. Intel 13th/14th Gen)

Maintainability

Can you upgrade RAM or Storage later if needed?

Battery (Laptops)

Does it have at least 50Wh battery for decent life?

Support

Does the brand have a service center near you?
