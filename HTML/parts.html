Master Your Machine

Learn about every component inside your computer. We decode the technical jargon so you can understand exactly what you're buying.

CPU (Processor)

Brain of the computer

The Central Processing Unit (CPU) is the most important component of your computer. It executes instructions and performs calculations that make your computer work. Think of it as the conductor of an orchestra.

  • Controls overall system speed and performance
  • Handles all computational tasks and processes
  • Determines how fast programs load and run
  • Affects multitasking capabilities

    • Pro Tip: Check Before Buying

    • Choose latest generation (Intel 13th/14th gen or AMD Ryzen 7000)
    • Minimum i5/Ryzen 5 for good performance
    • Consider core count (P-Cores vs E-Cores) for multitasking

    RAM (Memory)

    Runs multiple apps smoothly

    Random Access Memory (RAM) is your computer's short-term memory. It stores data that your CPU needs quick access to, enabling smooth multitasking. More RAM means more Chrome tabs!

  • Enables smooth swapping between applications
  • Determines how many programs run simultaneously
  • Influences overall system responsiveness

    • Pro Tip: Check Before Buying

    • 16GB is the "Gold Standard" for modern systems
    • 32GB+ recommended for video editing or 3D work
    • Look for DDR5 for future-proofing (faster speeds)

    Storage (SSD/HDD)

    Holds data, files, and software

    Storage devices keep your files, programs, and operating system permanently. SSDs (Solid State Drives) are modern, fast, and durable, while HDDs are older, slower, but cheaper for bulk storage.

  • Stores your operating system, programs, and files
  • NVMe SSDs provide instant boot and load times
  • Affects how quickly big files open and save

    • Pro Tip: Check Before Buying

    • ALWAYS choose an SSD for your main drive (C:)
    • 512GB is minimum, 1TB is comfortable
    • Look for "Gen 4 NVMe" for blazing speeds

    GPU (Graphics Card)

    Handles graphics, games, videos

    The Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) renders images, videos, and animations. While CPUs handle logic, GPUs handle visuals. It is the most critical component for gamers and video editors.

  • Renders all visual content on your screen
  • Essential for high-FPS gaming
  • Accelerates rendering in Premiere Pro, Blender, etc.

    • Pro Tip: Check Before Buying

    • Integrated graphics are fine for office work/Netflix
    • Get at least an RTX 4060 for modern gaming
    • Check VRAM (8GB+ recommended nowadays)

    Motherboard

    Connects all components together

    The motherboard is the backbone of your PC. It connects the CPU, memory, hard drives, optical drives, video card, sound card, and other ports and expansion cards directly or via cables.

  • Physical home for all your components
  • Determines CPU compatibility (Socket type)
  • Often includes Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and Audio

    • Pro Tip: Check Before Buying

    • Ensure socket matches CPU (e.g., LGA1700 for Intel)
    • "B" series boards (B760/B650) offer best value
    • Check for enough M.2 slots for storage expansion
