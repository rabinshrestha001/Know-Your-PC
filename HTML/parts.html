Parts Explained

Learn about all the components of laptops and PCs. Each part is explained in simple terms with tips on what to look for when buying.

CPU (Processor)

Brain of the computer

The Central Processing Unit (CPU) is the most important component of your computer. It executes instructions and performs calculations that make your computer work.

Key Features:

  • Controls overall system speed and performance
  • Handles all computational tasks and processes
  • Determines how fast programs load and run
  • Affects multitasking capabilities

Check Before Buying:

  • Choose latest generation (Intel 13th/14th gen or AMD Ryzen 7000)
  • Minimum i5/Ryzen 5 for good performance
  • Consider core count for multitasking

RAM (Memory)

Runs multiple apps smoothly

Random Access Memory (RAM) is your computer's short-term memory. It stores data that your CPU needs quick access to, enabling smooth multitasking.

Key Features:

  • Enables smooth multitasking between applications
  • Affects how many programs you can run simultaneously
  • Influences overall system responsiveness

Check Before Buying:

  • 16GB minimum recommended for modern systems
  • 32GB for professional work like video editing
  • Choose DDR4 or newer DDR5

Storage (SSD/HDD)

Holds data, files, and software

Storage devices keep your files, programs, and operating system. SSDs are much faster than traditional HDDs and significantly improve your computer's performance.

Key Features:

  • Stores your operating system, programs, and files
  • SSD provides much faster boot and load times
  • Affects how quickly programs and files open

Check Before Buying:

  • Always choose SSD over HDD for main storage
  • 1TB recommended for most users
  • Consider SSD + HDD combo for best value

GPU (Graphics Card)

Handles graphics, games, videos

The Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) renders images, videos, and animations. Essential for gaming, video editing, and graphics-intensive tasks.

Key Features:

  • Renders all visual content on your screen
  • Essential for gaming and video editing
  • Accelerates creative software performance

Check Before Buying:

  • Integrated graphics fine for basic office work
  • Dedicated GPU required for gaming and editing
  • Check power requirements (PSU Wattage)

Display

Your window to the digital world

The display is how you interact with your computer. Resolution, size, and panel type all affect your viewing experience and productivity.

Key Features:

  • Determines image clarity and sharpness
  • Panel type affects color accuracy (IPS/OLED)
  • Size impacts productivity and comfort

Check Before Buying:

  • Full HD (1920x1080) minimum resolution
  • IPS panel for better colors and viewing angles
  • Consider 144Hz+ for gaming

Motherboard

Connects all components together

The motherboard is the main circuit board that connects all components. It determines what parts you can use and affects future upgrade options.

Key Features:

  • Connects CPU, RAM, storage, and all components
  • Determines compatibility with future upgrades
  • Provides power distribution to all parts

Check Before Buying:

  • Match socket type with your CPU (Intel/AMD)
  • Ensure enough expansion slots for upgrades
  • Consider built-in Wi-Fi and Bluetooth

Power Supply (PSU)

Provides stable power to all components

The PSU converts AC power from your wall outlet to DC power that your computer components need. A quality PSU is crucial for system stability.

Key Features:

  • Distributes power to all components
  • Protects against power surges and fluctuations
  • Efficiency rating affects electricity costs

Check Before Buying:

  • Calculate total wattage needed (add 20% buffer)
  • Look for 80 Plus Bronze certification minimum
  • Modular cables make building easier

Cooling System

Keeps components at safe temperatures

Cooling systems prevent overheating and maintain optimal performance. Options include air cooling (fans and heatsinks) or liquid cooling for high-performance systems.

Key Features:

  • Prevents overheating and thermal throttling
  • Maintains consistent performance under load
  • Extends component lifespan

Check Before Buying:

  • Stock coolers fine for basic use
  • Aftermarket coolers for gaming and overclocking
  • Check case airflow and fan placement

Battery (Laptops)

Powers your laptop on the go

Laptop batteries determine how long you can work without being plugged in. Battery capacity, charging speed, and health are important factors.

Key Features:

  • Measured in Wh (Watt-hours) or mAh
  • Typical life: 4-12 hours depending on usage
  • Fast charging reduces downtime

Check Before Buying:

  • Minimum 50Wh for decent battery life
  • Look for fast charging support (USB-C PD)
  • Check real-world battery reviews

Ports & Connectivity

Connect to devices and networks

Ports and connectivity options determine what devices you can connect. Modern standards like USB-C, Wi-Fi 6, and Thunderbolt offer faster speeds.

Key Features:

  • USB ports for peripherals and storage
  • HDMI/DisplayPort for external monitors
  • Wi-Fi 6 for faster wireless speeds

Check Before Buying:

  • Minimum 2-3 USB ports (at least one USB-C)
  • HDMI essential for external displays
  • Thunderbolt 4 for high-speed data transfer
